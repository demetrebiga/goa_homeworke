BLACK HOLES

Black holes are some of the strangest and most fascinating objects in space. They're extremely dense, with such strong gravitational attraction that not even light can escape their grasp.
The Milky Way could contain over 100 million black holes, though detecting these gluttonous beasts is very difficult.
At the heart of the Milky Way lies a supermassive black hole — Sagittarius A*.
The colossal structure is about 4 million times the mass of the sun and lies approximately 26,000 light-years away from Earth, according to a statement from NASA.
The first image of a black hole was captured in 2019 by the Event Horizon Telescope (EHT) collaboration.
The striking photo of the black hole at the center of the M87 galaxy 55 million light-years from Earth thrilled scientists around the world.
They're huge concentrations of matter packed into very tiny spaces.
A black hole is so dense that gravity just beneath its surface, the event horizon, is strong enough that nothing not even light can escape.
The event horizon isn't a surface like Earth's or even the Sun's.
